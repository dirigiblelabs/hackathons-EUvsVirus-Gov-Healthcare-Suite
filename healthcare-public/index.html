Safety & Precautionary Measures

Avoid getting sick

The best way to prevent many people getting infected, which will lead to full hospitals, chaos and many deaths, is to avoid getting sick in the first place.


We strongly advise that you follow the WHO and CDC recommendations:



  • Wash your hands frequently with soap and water or clean them with an alcohol-based hand rub.
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth
  • Clean surfaces with alcohol-based wipes or spray containing at least 70% alcohol
  • Maintain social distancing
  • Stay home if you feel unwell
  • If you experience caugh shortness of breath and difficulty breathing seek medical help


WHO doesn’t recomment wearing a mask if you are healthy. If you are sick, however, wearing a mask is important as it can prevent you from invecting others.



Measures

Bulgaria is in a national state of emergency due 13 of May 2020 and haa put in place a series of measures in response to the coronavirus pandemic.

Measures include closing kindergarten, schools and universities as well as all cinemas, theaters, museums, clubs, restaurants and shops other than those selling food, pharmacies and bank offices.

Employers should introduce distance work whenever possible and if not, implement strong anti-epidemic measures, including disinfection of the premises and hygiene training for their employees. Bulgarians returning from abroad will be automatically assigned to quarantine for a period of 14 to 21 days. Violation of quarantine is punishable by jail and a fine.

Wearing a mask is compulsory for all citizens and is punishable by fine. Leaving or entering major cities is possible only with a declaration and due to urgent need work, health issues or returning to your permanent or current address

What should I do if I have Covid-19 symptoms

You may be infected If you experience the following symptoms:

  • fever above 38 degrees Celsius
  • dry cough
  • tiredness
  • sore throat
  • headache
  • Some patients may have aches and pains, nasal congestion, sore throat or diarrhea
  • Or have travelled to areas with epidemic risk in the last 14 days or people who have been in contact with a person diagnosed with or suspected of COVID-19 infection

If you think there is a chance that you may be infected you should:

  • Call your general practitioner
  • Isolate yourself from others
  • Wear a mask

How to test for Covid-19

There are 3 types of test for coronavirus:

  • PCR method (polymerase chain reaction) using a nose, throat or phlegm sample. According to experts, its results are the most reliable. This type of test is also recommended by the World Health Organization (WHO). The results are obtained within 48 hours of sampling.
  • Immunofluorescence test for an antigen that also proves the existence of a coronavirus in a throat sample. This is a quick test that takes 10-15 minutes and is used primarily for screening.
  • Antibody test - a blood sample is taken for this type of test. This study became popular as a "quick test" because the results range from minutes to hours. One of the antibodies being tested is immunoglobulin M (IgM) - if any, it means infection is coming soon. The other antibodies being tested are immunoglobulin G (IgG) - if any, it means the infection has passed and the person has healed. If both (IgM and IgG) are present, then the infection is 10 - 25 days old.

Everybody can test for coronavirus.

You can test with both PCR and antibody methods in (private) hospitals and private laboratories. The results of the samples tested, presented each morning by the Coronavirus Operations Headquarters, are from five laboratories: at the Military Medical Academy (MMA), the National Reference Laboratory of the National Center for Infectious and Parasitic Diseases, at St. Marina - Varna, at the University Hospital "Prof Dr. Kirkovich "- Stara Zagora and in a laboratory at the Medical University of Pleven.

Samples reach the four laboratories if you meet a combination of circumstances: if you have returned from a risk state and / or have been contacted (and referred to as Regional Health Inspectorate) with a proven COVID-19 infected person and / or have symptoms of coronavirus.

If you do not meet the criteria for a free trial, you can test yourself at any of the private hospitals and laboratories. Suspicious results are sent to the National Reference Laboratory.

Travel Restrictions

Entry and Exit Requirements:

  • The Bulgarian government has issued a ban on the entry of third-country (non-EU) nationals, including U.S. citizens, into the country. This also includes all travelers arriving from the following EU Member States and Schengen countries: Austria, Belgium, France, Germany, Iceland, Italy, Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, the Netherlands, Spain, Switzerland, and the United Kingdom including Northern Ireland. This includes all border crossing points with any means of transport, including aviation, maritime, rail, and road.
  • The ban does not apply to (a) Bulgarian citizens, family members of Bulgarian citizens, persons with permanent or long-term resident status in Bulgaria (and their family members); (b) healthcare professionals, health researchers and social workers traveling in performance of their professional duties; (c) transport personnel engaged in the carriage of goods, crews of aircraft engaged in commercial air transport and other transport personnel as necessary; (d) foreign officials, diplomats, officials of international organizations, military personnel and humanitarian workers in the performance of their duties; (e) persons traveling for humanitarian reasons; (f) nationals of Member States of the European Union and Schengen States (including San Marino, Andorra, Monaco and the Vatican) and third-country nationals directly involved in the construction, maintenance, operation and security of critical infrastructure in Bulgaria; (g) frontier workers and seasonal farm workers.
  • The order allows transit through Bulgaria for (a) nationals of EU and Schengen countries (plus their family members) when in transit to their country of residence, (b) third-country nationals holding long-term residence permits in another EU or Schengen country (plus their family members) when in transit to their country of residence; (c) citizens of Serbia, the Republic of Northern Macedonia, Montenegro and Turkey when in transit to their country of residence. In addition, transit is only permitted when the traveler will immediately depart Bulgaria and only when arranged in advance through a Bulgarian Embassy or Consulate outside Bulgaria.
  • Turkey, Serbia and North Macedonia have closed all border crossings to non-commercial traffic.
  • American citizens planning to depart Bulgaria should contact their airlines and consider contingency plans in case travel options become more limited in the near future.
  • International commercial flight options currently exist in Bulgaria. U.S. citizens who wish to return to the United States should make commercial arrangements as soon as possible unless they are prepared to remain abroad for an indefinite period. The U.S. government does not anticipate arranging repatriation flights in Bulgaria at this time.

Quarantine Information:

The quarantine period is 14 days. The main quarantine location for positively diagnosed and admitted patients is the Military Medical Academy in Sofia. Asymptomatic and mildly symptomatic patients should quarantine at home.
